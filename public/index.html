More About Scams

Stay informed. Understanding how scams work is your best defense.

🎣

Phishing Attacks

Scammers impersonate trusted companies via email, SMS, or fake websites to steal your login credentials, credit card numbers, or personal data. Always verify the sender's address and never click suspicious links.

📞

Vishing (Voice Phishing)

Fraudsters call pretending to be from banks, government agencies (like the IRS or RBI), or tech companies. They create panic about "suspicious activity" to extract sensitive information or payments.

🏆

Lottery & Prize Scams

You receive a message claiming you've won a big prize or lottery you never entered. The catch: you must pay a "processing fee" or share bank details. Legitimate lotteries never ask winners to pay upfront.

💸

Investment & Crypto Scams

Promises of guaranteed high returns, "risk-free" investments, or crypto multiplier schemes. If it sounds too good to be true, it is. Always verify with SEBI-registered advisors.

💼

Job Scams

Fake job offers requiring upfront "training fees" or "equipment deposits." Real employers never ask candidates to pay money. Verify company details on official websites before sharing personal information.

❤️

Romance Scams

Scammers create fake dating profiles and build emotional connections over weeks or months. Eventually they request money for "emergencies," "travel," or "medical bills." Never send money to people you haven't met in person.

🛡️

How to Protect Yourself

• Never share OTPs, passwords, or PINs
• Verify caller/sender identity independently
• Don't click links in unsolicited messages
• Enable 2FA on all accounts
• Report scams at cybercrime.gov.in

📱

UPI & Digital Payment Scams

Scammers send fake "collect request" links or QR codes. Remember: you never need to scan a QR code or enter your PIN to receive money. If someone asks you to do this, it's a scam.
